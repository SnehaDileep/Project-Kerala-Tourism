Kottayam

Kottayam is a district in the southernmost state of India, Kerala. Situated in Central Kerala, this district is bordered by the majestic Western Ghats to the east, the Vembanad Lake to the west and the paddy fields of Kuttanad to its unique features. The area is associated with numerous legends, with sprawling backwaters, lush paddy fields, hills, ridges, hills, and extensive rubber plantations. Kottayam is also known as the Land of Letters, Likes and Latex in English. Kottayam is a major trading center for cash crops, especially rubber. Most of the natural rubber in India is produced from well-maintained plantations per acre in Kottayam district.

What is kottayam famous for?

Ilaveezhapoonchira

Ilaveezhapoonchira is a tourist destination located in Melukavu village in Kottayam district near Kanjar. Ilaveezha Poonchira is surrounded by three enchanting hillocks - Mankunnu, Kodayathoormala and Thonippara

Illikkal Kallu

Illikkal Kallu is a monolith located on top of the Illickal Malaa in the Kottayam district of Kerala, India.The distance from kottayam railway station to illikal kallu is 57km.
